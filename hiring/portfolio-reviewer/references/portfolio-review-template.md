## Starter Example

Below is a concrete example of a completed case study evaluation and interview question. Use as a quality reference.

### Case Study 1: Checkout Redesign at ShopCo

**Context:** ShopCo (mid-market e-commerce, $50M ARR). Candidate was sole designer on a 6-person team. 3-month project. Shipped Q3 2025.

| Dimension | Score (1-4) | Evidence |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Problem Framing | 4 | Opens with cart abandonment data (68% drop-off at shipping step), cites 5 user interviews revealing address-form frustration. Clearly separates user problem from business problem. |
| Process & Rigor | 3 | Shows research → wireframes → testing → iteration. 3 rounds of user testing documented. Missing: no design explorations shown — jumps from wireframes to final design. |
| Craft Quality | 3 | Clean, systematic UI. Good use of spacing and typography. Error states and loading states shown. Minor: some inconsistency in button hierarchy across screens. |
| Systems Thinking | 2 | Responsive behavior not addressed. No mention of edge cases (international addresses, PO boxes, gift orders). No design system context. |
| Outcomes & Impact | 4 | "Cart completion rate increased from 32% to 47% within 6 weeks." Revenue impact calculated. Cites post-launch usability study confirming improvements. |
| Communication | 3 | Well-structured narrative. Good before/after comparisons. Slightly long — could be 30% shorter without losing substance. |
| Collaboration | 2 | Mentions "working with engineering" but no specifics. No evidence of PM collaboration, research partnership, or design critique. |

**Strongest dimension:** Problem Framing — rare to see a candidate lead with data AND user evidence.
**Weakest dimension:** Systems Thinking — missing responsive, edge cases, and system context is a concern at senior level.

### Interview Question Example

**Question 3:** "In the ShopCo checkout project, you show the final responsive design but I didn't see how you approached the mobile experience. Walk me through how you handled the shipping address form on mobile — what trade-offs did you make?"

- **What this probes:** Systems Thinking scored 2 — no responsive behavior in portfolio. Need to determine if this was done but not shown, or not considered.
- **Strong answer includes:** Specific mobile constraints addressed (thumb zones, keyboard types for address fields, autocomplete strategy), evidence of testing on mobile devices, trade-offs between mobile and desktop form layout.
- **Weak answer looks like:** "We just made it stack vertically on mobile" without evidence of mobile-specific design thinking.
