# Objective Writing Guide

Patterns and anti-patterns for writing effective objectives and key results.

## Objective Format

**Pattern:** "[Verb] [specific outcome] by [timeframe]"

**Good examples:**
- "Ship the Dashboard v2 design spec with full state coverage by April 30."
- "Reduce design QA defects by 40% by implementing a pre-handoff checklist."
- "Build stakeholder communication skills by independently leading 2 design presentations."

**Bad examples:**
- "Improve design quality." (vague — what quality? how measured?)
- "Be more strategic." (not actionable — what does strategic look like?)
- "Support the team." (not measurable — support how?)

## Key Result Patterns

### Metric-Based KRs
**Pattern:** "[Metric] moves from [baseline] to [target]."
- "Task completion rate for onboarding improves from 34% to 50%."
- "Design QA defects per sprint decrease from 8 to 3."
- "Stakeholder presentation satisfaction scores average 4+ out of 5."

### Artifact-Based KRs
**Pattern:** "[Deliverable] is completed and [accepted by / reviewed by / shipped]."
- "Design spec for Dashboard v2 is delivered and accepted by eng lead."
- "3 new design system components are documented and published."
- "Research synthesis for checkout is completed and presented to the product team."

### Observation-Based KRs
**Pattern:** "[Manager / peer / stakeholder] observes [specific behavior] in [context]."
- "Manager observes problem-first framing in all design reviews during Q2."
- "Eng lead confirms that handoff docs include all required states for 3 consecutive sprints."
- "2 peers provide feedback that design critiques are more actionable."

### Feedback-Based KRs
**Pattern:** "[Person] provides [specific type of] feedback confirming [outcome]."
- "Stakeholder provides positive feedback on presentation clarity after at least 1 presentation."
- "Mentee reports that pairing sessions were helpful in quarterly check-in."
- "PM confirms that design specs include measurable success criteria."

## Evidence Type Selection

| Evidence Type | Best For | Limitation |
|---------------|----------|------------|
| Metric | Delivery objectives with quantifiable outcomes | Requires instrumentation; may not capture design quality |
| Artifact | Tangible deliverables that can be reviewed | Doesn't measure quality without a reviewer |
| Observation | Behavioral changes and process improvements | Subjective; requires manager attention |
| Feedback | Influence, communication, and collaboration | Dependent on others providing it |

## Anti-Patterns

### Vague Objectives
- "Improve the user experience." → What experience? For whom? By how much?
- "Be a better collaborator." → What behaviors would demonstrate this?
- "Grow as a designer." → In what dimension? How will you know?

**Fix:** Add specificity until someone else could evaluate whether the objective was achieved.

### Uncontrollable Key Results
- "Increase sign-up conversion by 15%." → A designer can influence this but can't control it (marketing spend, pricing, market conditions all matter).
- "Get promoted to senior." → Promotion depends on headcount, timing, and calibration — not just performance.

**Fix:** Reframe around the work the report controls: "Deliver a redesigned sign-up flow that tests at 15% higher completion in A/B test" or "Demonstrate senior-level behaviors across all 7 competency dimensions."

### Checkbox Objectives
- "Attend 3 conferences." → Attendance ≠ learning.
- "Read 5 design books." → Reading ≠ application.
- "Complete the accessibility training." → Completion ≠ competence.

**Fix:** Focus on application: "Apply accessibility training by auditing 2 features using WCAG checklist and presenting findings to the team."

### Copy-Paste OKRs
- Taking a team OKR verbatim and assigning it to an individual without translation.
- "Team OKR: Launch Dashboard v2" → Individual: "Launch Dashboard v2."

**Fix:** Translate to the individual's scope and growth: "Lead design for Dashboard v2 core screens, delivering build-ready spec with full state coverage and success metrics defined."

### Growth Without Action
- "Improve stakeholder communication." → What will they DO?

**Fix:** Pair every growth objective with specific development actions: "Lead 2 stakeholder presentations. Shadow 1 VP-level review. Complete dry-run with manager before each presentation."

## Calibration Checklist

Before finalizing objectives, verify:
- [ ] 3-5 objectives total (not more).
- [ ] Mix includes delivery, craft, and growth.
- [ ] Every key result is measurable or verifiable.
- [ ] No key result depends on factors the report can't influence.
- [ ] Objectives are scoped to the report's current level (or level + 1 for stretch).
- [ ] At least one objective aligns to a team priority or org OKR.
- [ ] At least one objective connects to the report's career aspiration.
- [ ] Manager commitments are included.
- [ ] The report has reviewed and agreed to the objectives.
