# Objective Categories

Framework for balancing objectives across delivery, craft, and growth.

## The Three Categories

### Delivery (40-50% of objectives)
What the report ships and the impact it creates.

**Measures:** Project outcomes, feature launches, team contributions, stakeholder alignment.

**Examples by level:**
- **Junior:** "Ship the empty state designs for 3 dashboard widgets with all states documented."
- **Mid:** "Lead design for Dashboard v2 from discovery through spec, delivering a build-ready spec by [date]."
- **Senior:** "Define and ship the Q2 dashboard strategy, aligning product, eng, and design on scope and sequencing."
- **Staff+:** "Establish the design vision for the analytics platform across 3 product teams, with aligned roadmaps."

**Key results often use:** Artifact delivery, metrics impact, stakeholder approval, launch dates.

### Craft (20-30% of objectives)
The quality and rigor of their design work.

**Measures:** Design process quality, technical skill, system-level thinking, design quality improvements.

**Examples by level:**
- **Junior:** "Demonstrate proficiency in designing for all component states (default, empty, loading, error, disabled) in every project."
- **Mid:** "Improve problem framing by leading every design spec with a user-evidence-backed problem statement."
- **Senior:** "Raise the team's design quality bar by establishing a pre-handoff design QA checklist that catches 80% of state coverage gaps."
- **Staff+:** "Define and document 5 new interaction patterns for the design system that reduce component inconsistency across teams."

**Key results often use:** Observation, artifact quality assessment, peer feedback, before/after comparisons.

### Growth (20-30% of objectives)
The skills and capabilities they are developing.

**Measures:** Skill acquisition, leadership development, career progression, influence expansion.

**Examples by level:**
- **Junior:** "Build research skills by conducting 3 moderated user interviews with support from the research team."
- **Mid:** "Develop stakeholder communication by independently leading 2 design presentations this quarter."
- **Senior:** "Build mentorship capability by formally mentoring 1 junior designer through a full project cycle."
- **Staff+:** "Develop org-level influence by presenting the design strategy to the executive team and securing alignment."

**Key results often use:** Feedback, observation, artifacts produced, skills demonstrated.

## Level Mix Guidance

| Level | Delivery | Craft | Growth |
|-------|----------|-------|--------|
| Junior (IC1-2) | 30-40% | 30-40% | 30% |
| Mid (IC3) | 40% | 30% | 30% |
| Senior (IC4) | 50% | 20% | 30% |
| Staff+ (IC5+) | 50% | 20% | 30% |

**Notes:**
- Junior designers need more craft and growth because they're still building foundational skills.
- Senior+ designers have craft as a baseline expectation — their growth shifts toward leadership, influence, and strategy.
- Growth objectives should always connect to the report's stated career aspirations, not just team needs.
- Every cycle should have at least one objective in each category.

## Objective Count by Cycle Length

| Cycle | Recommended Count | Max |
|-------|-------------------|-----|
| Quarterly | 3-4 | 5 |
| Half-year | 4-5 | 6 |
| Annual | 5-6 | 7 |

More objectives = less focus. If you can't fit it in 5, you're overloading.
