# One-on-One Types

Reference for the canonical 1:1 types and their default structures. Use this to select the right agenda format.

## Weekly Check-In (Default)

**Duration:** 25-30 minutes
**Cadence:** Weekly
**Purpose:** Maintain connection, unblock, give/receive lightweight feedback, keep career goals alive.

**Default structure:**
1. Check-in (3 min) — energy, mood, top of mind
2. Report's topics (10 min) — their priorities first, always
3. Manager's topics (10 min) — feedback, alignment, context sharing
4. Growth & development (5 min) — one skill, goal, or learning
5. Action items (2 min) — commitments with owners and dates

**Manager mindset:** Listen more than talk. Aim for 70/30 report-to-manager ratio. Your job is to unblock and coach, not to status-check.

## Career Development

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Cadence:** Monthly or quarterly
**Purpose:** Discuss long-term growth, assess skill gaps, plan development, evaluate promotion readiness.

**Default structure:**
1. Check-in (5 min) — general state, relationship maintenance
2. Career reflection (15 min) — where they are now vs. where they want to be
3. Skill assessment (15 min) — strengths to double down on, gaps to close
4. Development plan (10 min) — specific actions, projects, or learning to pursue
5. Manager commitments (5 min) — what you will do to support their growth
6. Action items (5 min)

**Key prompts:**
- "What kind of work energizes you most right now?"
- "Where do you want to be in 12-18 months? What needs to change to get there?"
- "What skill, if you developed it, would have the biggest impact on your career?"
- "What's one thing I could do differently to better support your growth?"

**Manager mindset:** This is their conversation, not yours. Don't project your career path onto them. Ask, then listen.

## Performance Feedback

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Cadence:** As needed (not just during review cycles)
**Purpose:** Deliver specific, actionable feedback — positive or constructive — with clear expectations.

**Default structure:**
1. Frame the conversation (3 min) — set expectations for the discussion
2. Positive feedback (10 min) — specific strengths with SBI examples
3. Constructive feedback (15 min) — specific behaviors with SBI examples and expected change
4. Report's perspective (10 min) — their view, context you may be missing
5. Alignment on next steps (5 min) — what changes, by when, how you'll support
6. Action items (2 min)

**SBI Framework (Situation → Behavior → Impact):**
- "In [situation], when you [specific behavior], the impact was [specific outcome]."
- Always separate the behavior from the person. You're critiquing actions, not character.
- Include both positive and constructive examples — never deliver only negative feedback.

**Key prompts:**
- "I want to share some specific observations from the past few weeks. Can I walk you through them?"
- "Here's what I saw, here's the impact. Does that match your experience?"
- "What would you need from me to [make the change]?"

**Manager mindset:** Be direct, be specific, be kind. Vague feedback ("you need to be more strategic") is worse than no feedback. If you can't cite a specific example, you're not ready to give the feedback yet.

## Project Deep-Dive

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Cadence:** As needed (during complex projects)
**Purpose:** Go deep on design decisions, unblock strategic challenges, discuss stakeholder dynamics.

**Default structure:**
1. Project status (5 min) — where things stand, what's changed
2. Design decisions (15 min) — key trade-offs, alternatives considered, rationale
3. Challenges & blockers (10 min) — what's hard, what's stuck, where they need help
4. Stakeholder dynamics (5 min) — who's aligned, who's not, what to do about it
5. Next steps (5 min) — priorities for the next sprint/week

**Key prompts:**
- "Walk me through the hardest design decision you've made on this project. What were the alternatives?"
- "Where are you most uncertain? What would help you feel more confident?"
- "How's the relationship with [PM/Eng lead]? Any friction I should know about?"

**Manager mindset:** Coach, don't redesign. Your job is to help them think through decisions, not to make the decisions for them.

## Skip-Level

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes
**Cadence:** Monthly or quarterly
**Purpose:** Get unfiltered signal about team health, manager effectiveness, and individual career concerns.

**Default structure:**
1. Relationship building (5 min) — informal, human connection
2. Team health (10 min) — how the team is doing, what's working, what's not
3. Their experience (10 min) — career goals, support from their manager, any concerns
4. Open floor (10 min) — anything they want to share that they can't share elsewhere
5. Close (5 min) — thank them, clarify what you'll follow up on

**Key prompts:**
- "What's the best thing about being on this team right now?"
- "If you could change one thing about how the team works, what would it be?"
- "Do you feel like you're growing in your role? What would help?"
- "Is there anything you want to share that you haven't had a chance to?"

**Manager mindset:** You are not their manager — you are a safe escalation path. Never share specifics back to their manager without explicit permission. Act on patterns, not individual complaints.
