# Agenda Block Library

Modular blocks for assembling 1:1 agendas. Mix and match based on the 1:1 type and current context.

## Check-In Blocks

### Energy Pulse
**Duration:** 2-3 minutes
**When to use:** Every 1:1. Opens the conversation with a human check-in.
**Prompts:**
- "On a scale of 1-10, where's your energy this week?"
- "What's one word that describes your week so far?"
- "Anything on your mind before we dig in?"

### Wins & Gratitude
**Duration:** 3-5 minutes
**When to use:** When morale needs a boost, after a tough sprint, or to establish a positive tone.
**Prompts:**
- "What's one thing that went well since we last talked?"
- "Who on the team helped you this week? What did they do?"
- "What are you most proud of from the past sprint?"

### Stress Check
**Duration:** 3-5 minutes
**When to use:** When you suspect burnout, after high-intensity periods, or when the report has been unusually quiet.
**Prompts:**
- "How sustainable does your current workload feel?"
- "What's the thing that's weighing on you most right now?"
- "If I could take one thing off your plate, what would it be?"

## Report's Topics Block

### Open Floor
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes
**When to use:** Every weekly check-in. Report sets the agenda.
**Manager behavior:**
- Ask "What's on your mind?" and then stop talking.
- Take notes. Resist the urge to solve immediately.
- Ask "What would be most helpful from me?" before offering advice.

### Project Update
**Duration:** 10-15 minutes
**When to use:** During active projects when the report wants to discuss progress.
**Prompts:**
- "Where are you on [project]? What's ahead this week?"
- "What decisions are you making right now?"
- "Is there anything you need from me to stay unblocked?"

## Manager's Topics Block

### Specific Feedback (SBI)
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**When to use:** When you have a specific observation to share — positive or constructive.
**Framework:**
- **Situation:** "In Tuesday's design review..."
- **Behavior:** "...when you presented the three options with trade-offs for each..."
- **Impact:** "...the VP was able to make a decision in the meeting instead of requesting a follow-up."
**Follow-up:** "I want to see more of that. What helped you prepare that way?"

### Alignment & Context
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**When to use:** When there's organizational context the report needs, or when you need to align on priorities.
**Prompts:**
- "I want to share some context from [leadership meeting / planning session / reorg]."
- "Here's how I'm thinking about priorities for the team this quarter. How does that land for you?"
- "There's a change coming that will affect [project/team]. Here's what I know and what I don't."

### Expectation Setting
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**When to use:** When there's a gap between what you expect and what you're seeing, or when a new project starts.
**Prompts:**
- "For [project/behavior], here's what success looks like at your level: [specific]."
- "I want to make sure we're aligned on what 'done' looks like for [deliverable]."
- "This is what I need from you in the next [timeframe]: [specific]."

## Growth & Development Blocks

### Skill Spotlight
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**When to use:** Weekly, rotating through different skills.
**Prompts:**
- "What's one design skill you've stretched this week — even a little?"
- "Is there a skill you used this week that you'd like to develop further?"
- "What's the skill gap between where you are now and [next level]?"

### Career Visioning
**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**When to use:** Monthly career conversations.
**Prompts:**
- "When you think about the best version of your career in 2 years, what does your day look like?"
- "What kind of work do you want to be known for?"
- "If you got promoted tomorrow, what would you need to learn first?"
- "What project or opportunity would accelerate your growth the most right now?"

### Learning Review
**Duration:** 5-10 minutes
**When to use:** After a project ships, after training, or after a conference.
**Prompts:**
- "What's the biggest thing you learned from [project/event]?"
- "What would you do differently if you started that project over?"
- "How will you apply what you learned to your next project?"

## Coaching Question Patterns

Use these patterns to ask better questions in any block.

### Opening Questions
- "What's most important for us to talk about today?"
- "What do you need from this conversation?"
- "Where do you want to start?"

### Deepening Questions
- "Say more about that."
- "What makes that hard?"
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "What's the real challenge here for you?"

### Reframing Questions
- "What would you do if you knew you couldn't fail?"
- "What would [person you admire] do in this situation?"
- "What's the opposite of what you're considering — could that work?"

### Action Questions
- "What's the smallest next step you could take?"
- "What will you do by our next 1:1?"
- "Who else could help with this?"
- "What do you need from me to move forward?"

### Recognition Patterns
- "I noticed [specific behavior] and it had [specific impact]. Keep doing that."
- "That was a [level above] move — here's why: [specific]."
- "I want to call out [specific thing] because it demonstrates [growth area]."

## Anti-Patterns: What NOT to Do in 1:1s

- **Status meeting:** If you're asking "what did you do this week?" for every item, you're running a standup, not a 1:1.
- **Manager monologue:** If you're talking more than 30% of the time, you're not listening enough.
- **Surprise feedback:** Major constructive feedback should never be delivered for the first time in a 1:1 without warning. If it's serious, schedule a separate conversation.
- **Skipping 1:1s:** Canceling repeatedly signals "you're not important." Reschedule, don't cancel.
- **No follow-through:** If you commit to something in a 1:1, do it. Broken manager commitments erode trust fast.
- **Generic prompts only:** "How's it going?" is a fine opener, but if it's the only question you ask, you're not preparing.
